# Business Insights Report
## Executive Dashboard — Retail Sales Analysis (2024–2025)

---

## Overview

This document presents at least 8 actionable business insights derived from the executive dashboard. Each insight includes an observation, supporting data evidence, business interpretation, and a recommended action or follow-up question.

---

## Insight 1: Technology Dominates Revenue and Profit

**Observation:**
Technology is by far the most valuable product category in the portfolio.

**Data Evidence:**
- Technology Sales: ₹15.39 Crore (70.9% of total sales)
- Technology Profit: ₹2.80 Crore (84.2% of total profit)
- Technology Profit Margin: **18.2%**
- Furniture Margin: 6.9% | Office Supplies Margin: 14.9%

**Business Interpretation:**
Technology products generate the highest margins and contribute a disproportionately large share of total profit. The business is highly dependent on Technology for profitability. Furniture, despite moderate sales, yields low margins and drags overall profitability.

**Recommended Action:**
Prioritize inventory investment and marketing spend in Technology. Evaluate the Furniture category for cost optimisation or pricing adjustments. Consider reducing or phasing out low-margin Furniture sub-categories (e.g., Tables, Bookcases).

---

## Insight 2: High Discounts Severely Erode Profitability

**Observation:**
Orders with discounts of 30% or more are significantly less profitable than low-discount or no-discount orders.

**Data Evidence:**
- High Discount Orders (≥ 30%) Profit Margin: **6.5%**
- Low Discount Orders (< 10%) Profit Margin: **19.8%**
- Several orders with 30–35% discounts show **negative profit** (e.g., Furniture Tables and Bookcases)

**Business Interpretation:**
The discount strategy is creating value destruction in certain segments and categories. High discounts do not appear to drive enough volume to compensate for the margin loss — particularly in Furniture.

**Recommended Action:**
Introduce a discount cap policy — especially for Furniture — limiting discounts to a maximum of 20%. Run a profitability simulation to identify the optimal discount threshold per category. Redirect discount budgets toward loyalty or retention programs that don't require deep cuts.

## Insight 3: South Region Leads in Sales, East Region is Most Efficient

**Observation:**
The South region generates the highest revenue, but the East region delivers the best profit margin.

**Data Evidence:**
- South: Sales = ₹6.47 Crore, Profit = ₹99.9 Lakh, Margin = **15.4%**
- North: Sales = ₹5.46 Crore, Profit = ₹83.1 Lakh, Margin = **15.2%**
- East: Sales = ₹4.89 Crore, Profit = ₹76.0 Lakh, Margin = **15.6%**
- West: Sales = ₹4.89 Crore, Profit = ₹74.0 Lakh, Margin = **15.1%**

**Business Interpretation:**
While South leads in absolute sales volume, East achieves the highest profit efficiency. West lags slightly in margin despite similar sales volumes to East. The North region has strong sales potential but slightly underperforms on margin compared to South and East.

**Recommended Action:**
Study the East region's product mix and discount behaviour to understand why margins are higher. Apply those learnings to improve West region profitability. Investigate whether further market penetration in the North can boost margins without heavy discounting.

## Insight 4: Copiers, Accessories, and Phones Drive the Most Profit

**Observation:**
Three Technology sub-categories alone account for the majority of total profit.

**Data Evidence:**
- Copiers: Total Profit = ₹73.1 Lakh
- Accessories: Total Profit = ₹71.9 Lakh
- Phones: Total Profit = ₹70.9 Lakh
- Combined, these three sub-categories = ~65% of all profit

**Business Interpretation:**
The business has a concentrated profit engine in high-value Technology hardware. These products likely have strong demand, low return rates, and healthy pricing power with minimal discounting.

**Recommended Action:**
Protect pricing integrity for these three sub-categories — avoid applying heavy discounts. Ensure strong stock availability, especially during high-demand periods. Consider upsell or bundle strategies (e.g., Copiers + Accessories) to increase average order value.

## Insight 5: Home Office Segment Has the Highest Return Rate

**Observation:**
The Home Office customer segment returns orders more frequently than Corporate or Consumer segments.

**Data Evidence:**
- Home Office Return Rate: **5.7%**
- Corporate Return Rate: **4.0%**
- Consumer Return Rate: **3.9%**
- Home Office Sales: ₹7.45 Crore (highest of the three segments)

**Business Interpretation:**
Despite being the top-selling segment by revenue, Home Office customers are returning products at a materially higher rate. This may indicate product-expectation mismatches, quality perception issues, or shipping damage in smaller-scale orders.

**Recommended Action:**
Analyse which specific products Home Office customers return most frequently. Review product descriptions and images for accuracy. Implement a targeted post-purchase support program (e.g., onboarding emails, how-to guides) for Home Office orders to reduce returns. Follow-up question: Are returns concentrated in a specific category or region for this segment?

## Insight 6: Standard Class Shipping Has the Highest Average Delay

**Observation:**
Standard Class is the most commonly used shipping mode but carries the longest average delivery time.

**Data Evidence:**
- Standard Class: avg. delivery = **4.7 days**, 2,435 orders (58% of all orders)
- First Class: avg. delivery = **1.8 days**, 630 orders
- Second Class: avg. delivery = **2.7 days**, 894 orders
- Same Day: avg. delivery = **0.4 days**, 241 orders

**Business Interpretation:**
More than half of all orders are shipped via Standard Class with nearly a 5-day average delivery window. This may be causing customer dissatisfaction, especially when customers expect faster fulfilment in the e-commerce era.

**Recommended Action:**
Evaluate whether the cost savings from Standard Class are worth the potential customer satisfaction impact. Consider offering Free/Discounted First Class shipping above a spend threshold to incentivise larger orders while improving delivery experience. Monitor customer ratings by shipping mode to check correlation.

## Insight 7: Furniture Tables and Bookcases Consistently Generate Losses

**Observation:**
Several orders in the Tables and Bookcases sub-categories result in negative profit.

**Data Evidence:**
- Orders with discount ≥ 30% in Tables sub-category show profits such as -₹1,582, -₹1,695, -₹1,806
- Bookcases with 30% discount show losses: -₹3,335, -₹3,134, -₹46
- Furniture overall margin is only **6.9%** — heavily dragged by these sub-categories

**Business Interpretation:**
Tables and Bookcases are loss-making when discounts are applied at or above 30%. These sub-categories likely have high logistics costs (bulky items) but are being discounted aggressively, making individual orders unprofitable.

**Recommended Action:**
Set a hard discount floor of 15% for Tables and Bookcases. Review cost structures for bulky furniture items — if shipping costs are high, factor them into minimum pricing. Consider whether these sub-categories should remain in the product portfolio or be repositioned as premium offerings.

## Insight 8: Organic Channel Drives the Most Orders; Paid Channel Should Be Evaluated for ROI

**Observation:**
The Organic channel is the dominant source of orders, while Paid channels represent a meaningful but smaller share.

**Data Evidence:**
- Organic is the most common campaign channel across all regions and segments
- Paid, Email, Social, and Referral are secondary channels
- Null/missing campaign channel exists for some orders — suggesting untracked attribution

**Business Interpretation:**
The business benefits from strong organic demand, reducing reliance on paid marketing. However, the presence of significant Paid channel spend warrants an ROI analysis — are paid campaigns generating sufficiently profitable orders to justify spend?

**Recommended Action:**
Build a channel-level profitability view in the dashboard: compare average profit margin and return rate by campaign channel. Investigate whether Paid channel orders are more likely to include heavy discounts. Fix attribution tracking for orders with null campaign channel to improve future analysis accuracy.

## Insight 9 (Bonus): Sales Trend Shows Strong 2025 Growth

**Observation:**
Orders placed in 2025 (order IDs starting with DB-2025) appear to be at higher average sales values than 2024 orders.

**Data Evidence:**
- 2024 and 2025 orders are both represented in the dataset
- Several of the highest-value individual orders (₹2.7–3.4 Lakh) appear in the 2025 cohort
- The dataset covers a ~2-year window, allowing trend comparison

**Business Interpretation:**
If average order values are rising in 2025, this could reflect product mix shift toward higher-value Technology items, successful upselling, or price increases passing through successfully.

**Recommended Action:**
Build a monthly/quarterly sales trend line chart in Tableau to confirm and quantify year-over-year growth. Decompose by category to identify which product lines are driving the growth. If growth is concentrated in Technology, ensure supply chain and inventory can support continued demand.

## Calculated Fields Used in These Insights

| Field | Formula |
|---|---|
| Profit Margin | `SUM([Profit]) / SUM([Sales])` |
| Cost | `SUM([Sales]) - SUM([Profit])` |
| Average Order Value | `SUM([Sales]) / COUNTD([Order ID])` |
| Return Rate | `SUM([Return Flag]) / COUNT([Order ID])` |
| Shipping Delay Bucket | `IF [Delivery Days] = 0 THEN "Same Day" ELSEIF [Delivery Days] <= 2 THEN "Fast (1–2 days)" ELSEIF [Delivery Days] <= 4 THEN "Standard (3–4 days)" ELSEIF [Delivery Days] <= 6 THEN "Slow (5–6 days)" ELSE "Very Slow (7+ days)" END` |
| Discount Category | `IF [Discount] = 0 THEN "No Discount" ELSEIF [Discount] < 0.2 THEN "Low (< 20%)" ELSEIF [Discount] < 0.3 THEN "Medium (20–29%)" ELSE "High (30%+)" END` |
